Also, today, I bricked my PC. I stayed up from 10 PM till 4 AM, working my arse off to fix it before my Zoom lecture at ~8PM. I got it fixed. I installed Windows, then Archlinux again.

Apparently, writing the image in either ISO (ESP) or ISO mode makes it keep everything in a cache which makes installing packages impossible on the USB image -- so use DD mode, for those of you Rufus fans out there.

I used the MBR scheme because... I like MBR. Sue me. (I have a non-CSM and UEFI-supported PC, but MBR takes the cake.) Anyway, Windows also failed to install because when I tried to install Archlinux yesterday, it broke the MBR & Boot record! Yay, so fun.

...And that broke everything else. I had to fully reinstall & format the C:\ drive, resulting in a loss of programs, however, there is a pro to being a huge data-hoarder. Having like 10 storage devices means you can store your programs freely on the USBS/portable-SSD/HDDs. Useful!
